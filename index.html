About Me

Hi there! I'm Fang, a third-year undergraduate student majoring in Mathematics and Statistics at University of Michigan.

I'm currently working as an LLM Researcher in Statistics department, gratefully advised by Prof.Yixin Wang and Zhiwei Xu.

My research interests focus on investigating the weak-to-strong generalization capabilities of large language models and uncovering the fundamental laws that sustain their self-improvement.

As a former Data Analyst Intern at AstraZeneca, I led sales performance analytics and developed Power BI dashboards that streamlined therapy prioritization for over 30,000 healthcare institutions.

Research

Self-Improving Transformers Approach the Generalization Limit

A Governing Law of Sustainability and Collapse

Fang Yu, Zifei Bai, Zhiwei Xu, Yixin Wang

We explore the weak-to-strong generalization capabilities of LLMs and developing self-improving transformer frameworks that enhance performance on tasks like arithmetic and string copying using unsupervised majority voting and length filtering. Our work aims to understand how these iterative self-improving processes scale with increasing task complexity and to derive a governing law prediction when the model might collapse.
